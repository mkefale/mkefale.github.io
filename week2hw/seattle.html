First Stop: Seattle

After moving back from New York into my Evanston apartment, I set off for Seattle. Two of my closest friends, Sean and Kailey, moved there about a year ago and I hadn't seen either of them since.

I quickly learned the great thing about Seattle is how much there is to do. Sean and I spent my first day checking all the tourist-y boxes, and then the three of us went for a hike in the Snoqualmie Mountains on Saturday - followed by a VERY lazy Sunday.

Pike Place Market? Check.

Behold the infamous "gum wall," which Sean pointed out had been scraped clean just a couple months prior. We did not contribute to this insanity.

View from the Waterfront

This was considered a "cloudy" day in Seattle because you couldn't see the Cascade Mountains clearly in the horizon at midday. So this was a little preview of the next day's adventures.

The Space Needle! (Kind of)

By the time Sean and I made our way to the Space Needle, the wait was nearly two hours. NOPE. Instead, we walked to this water fountain, whose spouts are synchronized to classical music. We tried not to laugh too hard at the playing kids who got sprayed in the face.

The Great Outdoors

Our Saturday hike wasn't a long one, but it had a great payoff!
You can spot Sean in the corner getting his own picture of this gorgeous waterfall.

Into The Woods

The thing I love about hiking in the mountains is that the journey is just as beautiful as the payoff.

Little Treasures

Kailey found tons of these little crystals in the riverbed. She saved most of them to give to her friend Jennie, who likes to make her own jewelry, but I got to keep this one.
