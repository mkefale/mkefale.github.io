On My Own with So Much Food: Portland

I included Portland in my West Coast journey for two reasons: 1) I've never been and 2) my former coworker Erica was planning to move there in early August. Except Erica decided in July to extend at work for a couple more months, so I found myself three weeks out from landing in Portland with no place to stay.

AirBnB to the rescue! I found a little garage-converted-into-studio off Portland's SE Division St and had a 36-hr solo adventure in a brand new city. How do you kill time alone in a new place? If you're me, by eating and drinking everything in walking distance.

Burning Off The Future Calories

One of my favorite things about vacation is getting to sleep in and still exercise. I went for a morning run to a historic reservoir to familiarize myself with the neighborhood - and prepare for my food-filled day.

Lunch + Drinks + Dessert

Look at all the things I ate (and drank)!! That ice cream in the bottom corner was definitely a highlight: strawberry honey balsamic with black pepper, a fan favorite from Salt & Straw.

Rome By Way of Portland

Ava Gene's is a local staple, serving up high-end Italian dishes that still taste like comfort food. My main dish (bottom) was a hearty lasagna, while my appetizer (top) was an airy pastry, served with proscuitto and covered with parmigiano, chili flakes, and honey. Totally worth the last-minute 5:30 PM reservation.
